Another Future

What if the COVID global pandemic extends for decades?
Will social distancing become part of the next humanity?

Another Future is an open-ended architectural fiction driven by a series of loosely linked stories in a(nother) post-2020 world.

Tip: you may find better experience on mobile screens with motion/orientation sensors enabled for panorama/VR scenes;

[2020] COVID-19 Outbreak

COVID-19 OUTBREAK

×

The pandemic era begins with COVID-19 sweeping across the globe;

Urban routines of working/ schooling/ shopping/ recreation/ social life turn into remote forms under social distancing requirements, where Zoom and Amazon become significant social infrastructures;

Digital gaps reveal between the industries advanced of remote operation and the majority others requiring onsite work, which also widens economic gaps between the corresponding laborforce;

Public health measures become a contesting ground for economic, political, cultural, and spatial conflicts from individual to international scales;

The paused social production also intensifies the historically established racial and political tensions;

Hurricanes, wildfires, floods, and freezes under 'unprecedented' climates add additional strikes to the stay-home populations of North America and Southeast Asia;

[2021--] COVID Normalization

COVID NORMALIZATION

×

The continuous fights between mutative COVID developments and corresponding public health actions gradually legitimize new urban norms of regulated social distancing practice, periodical vaccination reinforcements, and graded lockdowns for different scenarios (L1 for masked access to public space - L5 for absolute quarantine);

Internet evolves into a placeless collection of workplaces, schools, shopping sites, social clubs, amusement parks, courts, and voting boxes that collectively reformulate one's work-live habits, whereas cities and local communities virtually reduce to public health units for COVID-related regulations and actions;

Public investments in digital production and service grow under the drastic work-life transitions; online exchange of information and material become fundamental skills for human life; internet access becomes a formally acknowledged human right;

Digital gaps between industries and corresponding workforces evolve into spatial segregations between the 'safer' communities of digital workers with controlled access and the 'risky' commuter's neighborhoods with unstable social distancing practice, which can often turn into 'unemployed neighborhoods' during lockdowns;

[-2030-] 5.0 Technologies

5.0 TECHNOLOGIES

×

The COVID transitions of social production stimulate economic and political investments in the global network of 5.0 Infrastructures:

- Internet 5.0:
the pre-pandemic standard of 5G cellular network that has been reshaped and made globally available through a low-orbit satellite matrix under the US-China 5G competition, providing uninterrupted digital access for people at home and machines across borders; ;

- Industry 5.0:
a comprehensive system of remote collaboration between digital workers and machinery that enables fully unmanned industrial activities through automated procedures and remote human interventions; relevant technologies are initially developed to sustain the productivity of primary industries during COVID lockdowns and then been widely adopted in other industrial businesses of various scales;

- Mobility 5.0:
the pre-pandemic visions of Level 5 autonomous vehicle that has been standardized for driverless forms of air, ground, and aquatic transportations, thanks to the restricted personal mobility that helps their training and legislation in most cities as well as heavy public investments in relevant services such as Amazon and Uber Air;

On the other hand, oppositions rise among the digitally disadvantaged societies and stakeholders of human-labored industries with major concerns regarding employment opportunities, techno-monopolies, and cultural identities of production;

[STORY] NEXT FLOOR

NEXTFLOOR

NEXT FLOOR

Tokyo, JP, 2032

×

Home Extension began a year ago when someone discovered a genius way to make 'outdoor sections' out of our 15-square-meter pencil building apartments for legal visits during Tokyo's L4 lockdown. Since then we made ourselves an offline version of SimHouse by furnishing part of our rooms for particular interests and sharing with everyone in the building. Despite having dividers between the host and guest for the one-per-room baseline, I nevertheless rediscovered the pre-pandemic vibe by visiting my neighbors on a daily basis. The only person absent from our program is the 'ghost 13F' living upstairs...


[2030--] Nomads and Commuters

NOMADS AND COMMUTERS

×

5.0 Technologies thrives across diverse industries and services under the significant strike of Sigma variant that imposes L5 lockdowns across the world. Digital economies rapidly occupy the less-inhabitable lands and waters to built unmanned production facilities; digital workers adopt the title of 'nomad' for spending most of their time and energy somewhere across screens while living in resort-like communities with limited physical exposures to the COVID-saturated world outside;

On the other hand, the digital divisions intensify as digital economies continuously drive out human-labored industries while providing limited employment slots to rebalance social structures. Apart from the intensified competition in digital entrepreneurship and online job markets across borders, a majority of urban populations remain under the title of 'commuter' for having physical workplace with unavoidable commuting and interpersonal contacts. While most countries of the global south sign market protection acts against foreign digital invasions, digitally developed countries also establish industrial reservations to reconcile social conflicts between the two distinctive populations. On the other hand, the inability to perform social distancing makes labor-intense industries perfect incubators for COVID mutations and variant developments, which in turn destabilize the employment and commonwealth of commuter societies;

[STORY] INSTANT OLDTOWN

INSTANTOLDTOWN

INSTANT OLDTOWN

Tallinn, Estonia, 2040

×

Although Tallinn has been my favorite COVID-free town for its Medieval cultures, I rarely related it to my E-Estonian business before the 90-day-annual visiting requirement came in force. I'm not sure about the rumors on the country trying to convert E-Estonians to geo-Estonians for demographic compensation, but I can see how Tallinn comes alive every summer when nomads land at Old City Harbor, light up Baltic Quarantine Resort for their arrival week, and dive into their oldtown dreams with #negative tags on.
However, these procedures run a bit different for me this year...


[-2040-] Drifting Cities

DRIFTING CITIES

×

The resumed industrial activities under 5.0 Technologies accelerate the global warming progress, causing not only 'searises' (a.k.a. the sea-level-rise) to flood coastal cities earlier than expected but also the intensified COVID mutations as combined with the once-frozen pathogens from melted polar ice.

By 2040, citis of 140 million populations have been affected by searises with low-land areas flooded and urban infrastructures exposed to erosions, creating significant waves of urban migrations from coasts to inland cities that accelerate COVID transmissions in affected regions;

[2050] United Digital Nations

UNITED DIGITAL NATIONS

×

The 5.0-equipped digital economies raise political tensions on cross-border laborforce, taxation, and market regulation, calling an invisible war against geopolitical orders of global economy. In addition to speculative interventions of digital giants to domestic markets, the digital transition also popularize tax-driven immigration and employment arbitrages among nomads which further dilute the notion of citizen and residency in social production;

The 2035 UN World Summit initiates United Digital Nations (UDN) as an intergovernmental division to reinforce geopolitical power structures through collaborative regulations on cross-border productions, digital markets, jurisdictions, and taxations; on the other hand, the interwoven interests between speculative politics and digital economies often appropriate the roles of UDN into a borderless alliance for nomadic capitals;
