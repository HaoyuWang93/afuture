Another Future

What if the COVID pandemic continuously develops toward a distant future?
What if social distancing practice pushes technologies of remote work toward their maximized capacities?

Another Future is my ongoing experiment towards an architectural fiction.
It takes an open-ended imagination of the Post-2020 world to a series of loosely linked stories.
I will keep posting new ideas, information, and stories to extend this work.
Please feel free to reach out for questions and ideas.

Tip: you may find your mobile screen a better fit for panoramic scenes in the stories,
as no one would blame you for turning back and forth when you practice social distancing (¬‿¬)

[2020] COVID-19 Outbreak

COVID-19 OUTBREAK

×

The pandemic era begins with COVID-19 sweeping across the globe,
remote adaptations of work/ school/ shopping/ service/ social life shifts from a lifestyle to a MUST under social distancing requirements;

[2021-] COVID Normalization

COVID NORMALIZATION

×

The continuous fights between COVID mutations and public health measures normalize social distancing practice, seasonal vaccination reinforcements, and periodical lockdowns in most urban societies;

[2030-] 5.0 Technologies

5.0 TECHNOLOGIES

×

The COVID transitions of social production stimulate economic and political investments on 5.0 Infrastructures:
- 5G Cellular Network for data transmission;
- Industry 5.0 for unmanned production;
- Lv. 5 Mobility for material flows;

[2035-] Digital Gaps

DIGITAL GAPS

×

Digital production thrives across diverse industries and services under the dual impacts of COVID developments and 5.0 Technologies, amplifying the social gaps between populations of digital workers and onsite workers (or commuters);

While the unmanned production sites occupy less-inhabitable lands and waters to advance their productivities at energy thresholds, their limited hiring slots leave most populations to industrial reservations by political acts with unstable employment under COVID impacts, let alone the significant number of unemployed living on rations;

Digital economies also challenge the relationships between workforce and residency, giving birth to United Digital Council (UDC) as a regulating force on cross-border productions, consumptions, jurisdictions, and taxations;

[-2040-] Drifting Cities

DRIFTING CITIES

×
The resumed industrial activities under 5.0 Technologies accelerate the global warming progress, causing not only sea-level-rises to flood coastal cities earlier than expected but also intensified COVID mutations as combined with the once-frozen pathogens from melted polar ice.

The recurring disasters of extreme climates, sea-rises, COVID lockdowns, and their accompanied social disorders increasingly destabilize urban societies;

[STORY] HOME PORT

HOMEPORT

HOME PORT

×

'...so why did you move out of campus?'
This recurring question comes from not only my digital worker fellows but also my social-distanced neighbors here in South Boston's submerged zone. As the Omega variant calls another victory of COVID against our latest vaccines, campus towers seem to be the only places free from the next inevitable lockdowns. When Campus Group initially advertised their unsinkable vertical city concept to the sea-rise victims, they probably didn't believe the towers' controlled access to be the biggest selling point in the face of Omega.

It is true that as digital workers, our expectations for physical life lie on the leisure side. So we support controlled access to campus and any tracking measures inside, as long as we can have endless parties beside remote work while people outside struggle against COVID for their onsite jobs. On the other hand, I somehow smelt another level of lockdown framed by the tower's COVID-PASS infrastructure when watching service drones circled around for portal scanning sequence and hologram ads redirecting my walk to a 'healthier' lunch spot. My incremental fears for the next PASS updates culminated with my escape from campus and eventually led me to South Boston where I can live untrackable under masks and no longer be socially distanced from the entire city.

However, this life is about to change when DPH announces the city's next response to the upcoming Omega...
