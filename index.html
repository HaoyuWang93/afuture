Another Future

What if the COVID pandemic extends for decades?
Will remote work dominate social production across industries?

Another Future is an open-ended architectural fiction that accommodates and proceeds with a series of loosely linked stories in a post-2020 world.

I will keep posting new stories and info updates to the timeline.

[2020] COVID-19 Outbreak

COVID-19 OUTBREAK

×

The pandemic era begins with COVID-19 sweeping across the globe;

Urban routines of working/ schooling/ shopping/ recreation/ social life turn into remote forms under social distancing requirements, where Zoom and Amazon become significant social infrastructures;

Digital gaps reveal between the industries eligible for work-at-home transitions and the majority rest requiring onsite work, which also widens social gaps between the laborforce of relevant professions;

Public health measures become a contesting ground for economic, political, cultural, and spatial conflicts from individual to global scales; China and South Korea practice large-scale lockdowns and tracking actions down to individual personnel while Western societies present significant controversies on social distancing policies;

The paused social production also intensifies the established social tensions of employment rates, wealth inequities, racial discrimination, and speculative politics, as comprehensively demonstrated by incidents of USA under Trump Administration;

Hurricanes, wildfires, floods, and freezes under 'unprecedented' climates add another strike to the stay-home populations of North America and Southeast Asia;

[2021-] COVID Normalization

COVID NORMALIZATION

×

The continuous fights between mutative COVID developments and corresponding public health actions gradually shape new urban norms of regulated social distancing practice, periodical vaccination reinforcements, and graded lockdowns for different scenarios (L0 for masked access to public space - L5 for absolute quarantine);

Digital space evolves into a collection of workplaces, schools, shopping sites, social clubs, amusement parks, courts, and voting boxes, that accommodates the majority of one's work and life, whereas cities and local communities reduce to public health units powered by their Department of Public Health (DPH) to regulate COVID measures;

Public investments in digital production, service, and infrastructure grow under the drastic work-life transitions; information and material exchanges online become fundamental skills for individuals; internet access becomes a formally acknowledged human right;

Digital gaps between industries and corresponding workforces evolve into spatial segregations between the 'safer' communities of digital workers with controlled access and the commuter's neighborhoods that are regarded as COVID-saturated and can often become 'unemployed neighborhoods' during lockdowns;

The increasing online employment, education, and commercial activities raise political tensions on cross-border laborforce, taxation, and market regulation, initiating the great war between the local protection acts of geopolitics and foreign digital invasions;

USA, Russia, and EU troops retreat from battlefields for reduced expenses under economic hardship; however, their military interventions remain active in the Middle East, North Africa, and Central Aisa in forms of proxy wars on relevant interests;

[-2030-] 5.0 Technologies

5.0 TECHNOLOGIES

×

The COVID transitions of social production stimulate economic and political investments in the global network of 5.0 Infrastructures:

- Internet 5.0:
the pre-pandemic standard of 5G cellular network that has been reshaped and made globally available through the low-orbit satellite matrix constructed by tech giants of USA and China, providing uninterrupted digital access for people at home and machines across borders;

- Industry 5.0:
a comprehensive system of remote collaboration between digital workers and machinery that has been developed in Germany and Japan and widely adopted in global economy, enabling fully unmanned production under remote controls and automated procedures to recover industrial productivity under persistent COVID impacts;

- Mobility 5.0:
the pre-pandemic visions of Level 5 autonomous vehicle that has been standardized for driverless forms of air, ground, and aquatic transportations, thanks to the restricted personal mobility that helps the legislation for driverless zones in cities as well as heavy public investments in relevant services such as Amazon and Uber Air;

On the other hand, oppositions rise among the digitally disadvantaged societies and stakeholders of onsite laborforces with major concerns regarding employment opportunities, techno-monopolies, and cultural identities of production;

[-2035-] Digital Divisions

DIGITAL DIVISIONS

×

5.0 Technologies thrives across diverse industries and services under the significant strike of Sigma variant on human-labored industries. While commuters have to maintain a mobile COVID-PASS by performing infection scans at most door-to-door scenarios for their everyday onsite work, digital workers enjoy uninterrupted employment at home; the spatial separation between two populations continues to evolve, where commuters accumulate at hiring sites while digital workers relocate to resort-like suburban communities or urban towers that vertically separated from 'risky' zones;

Digital economies rapidly occupy the less-inhabitable lands of Saraha, Siberia, West Australia, Tibet, and remote islands on open waters to built unmanned production facilities for food and industrial products; on the other hand, the limited energy resource and depressed consumer markets support few active slots for digital workforces. The majority of populations, especially in the digitally disadvantaged societies of Global South, rely on market protection acts to remain productive under continuous COVID impacts. Developed countries also establish industrial reservations to reconcile the social conflicts brought by digital divisions. On the other hand, the inability to perform social distancing makes labor-intense industries perfect COVID incubators for mutations and variant developments, which in turn destabilize the employment and commonwealth of onsite working populations;

[2030] United Digital Nations

UNITED DIGITAL NATIONS

×

The 5.0-equipped digital economies continuously challenge the geopolitical identities of digital workforces, which not only intensify speculative interventions of tech giants in the prolonged US-China economic competitions but also popularize tax-driven immigration and remote-employment arbitrages in the growing population of digital workers that further dilute the notion of citizen and residency in social production;

As digital giants extend their economic powers in a placeless market beyond geopolitical orders, G7 and China co-initiated the United Digital Nations (UDN) to reinforce existing power structures through intergovernmental regulations on cross-border productions, consumptions, jurisdictions, and taxations;

[-2040-] Drifting Cities

DRIFTING CITIES

×

The resumed industrial activities under 5.0 Technologies accelerate the global warming progress, causing not only sea-level-rises to flood coastal cities earlier than expected but also the intensified COVID mutations as combined with the once-frozen pathogens from melted polar ice.

By 2040, citis of 140 million populations have been affected by sea-rise with low-land areas flooded and urban infrastructures exposed to erosion, creating significant waves of urban migrations from coasts to inland cities, which accelerate COVID transmissions on their traces and destabilize the public health measures of their destinations;

[STORY] HOME PORT

HOMEPORT

HOME PORT

Boston, MA, 2045

×

'...so why did you move out of campus?'

This recurring question comes from not only my digital fellows but also my social-distanced neighbors here in the submerged zone of South Boston. As the Omega variant calls another victory of COVID against vaccines, campus towers seem to be the only places free from the next inevitable lockdowns. Campus Group probably won't believe that their vertical city concept would sell this year for controlled access rather than being 'unsinkable' as they initially advertised to sea-rise victims.

It's true that as digital workers, our expectations for physical beings lie on the leisure side. So we support controlled access to campus and any tracking measures inside, as long as we can have endless parties while people outside struggle against COVID for their onsite jobs. On the other hand, I somehow smelt another level of lockdown framed by the COVID-PASS infrastructure when watching service drones circled around for portal scanning sequence and hologram ads redirecting my walk to a 'healthier' lunch spot downstairs. My incremental fears for the next PASS updates culminated with my escape from campus and eventually led me to South Boston, where I can live untrackable under masks and no longer be socially distanced from the real city.

However, this life is about to change when DPH announces the city's next response to the upcoming Omega...
