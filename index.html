Another Future

...seems like the COVID pandemic is becoming another end-of-world event to be forgived, with some minor twists bringing us back to normal or the next threats coming into play;
What if, in an exceptional case, the pandemic (lethally) extended for decades?
What if social distancing became part of the next humanity?

Another Future is an interactive architectural fiction propelled by a series of loosely linked stories in a(nother) post-2020 world.

Tip: you may find better experience on a mobile screen with motion sensor enabled, as most stories involve exploring 360Â° scenes (Web-VR) in first-person perspective and tapping buttons/ interactive objects to proceed;

[2020] COVID-19 Outbreak

COVID-19 OUTBREAK

×

The pandemic era begins with COVID-19 sweeping across the globe;

Urban routines of working/ schooling/ shopping/ recreation/ social life begin their digital transitions under social distancing requirements, where Zoom and Amazon become significant social infrastructures;

Digital gaps reveal between the industries advanced of remote operation and the majority others requiring onsite work, which also widens economic gaps between relevant communities;

Public health measures become a contesting ground for economic, political, cultural, and spatial conflicts from individual to international scales;

The paused social production also intensifies the historically established racial and political tensions;

Hurricanes, wildfires, floods, and freezes under 'unprecedented' climates add additional strikes to the stay-home populations of North America and Southeast Asia;

[2021--] COVID Normalization

COVID NORMALIZATION

×

The continuous fights between mutative COVID developments and corresponding public health actions gradually legitimize new urban norms of regulated social distancing practices, periodical vaccination reinforcements, and graded lockdowns for different scenarios (L1 for masked access to public space - L5 for absolute quarantine);

Internet evolves into a placeless collection of workplaces, schools, shopping sites, social clubs, amusement parks, courts, and voting boxes that collectively reformulate one's work-live habits, whereas cities and local communities virtually reduce to public health units for COVID-related regulations and actions;

Public investments in digital production and service grow under the drastic work-life transitions; online exchange of information and material become fundamental skills for human life; internet access becomes a formally acknowledged human right;

Digital gaps between industries and corresponding workforces evolve into spatial segregations between the 'safer' communities of digital workers with controlled access and the 'risky' neighborhoods of urban commuters with unstable social distancing practice;

[2032] NEXT FLOOR

NEXTFLOOR

NEXT FLOOR

Tokyo, JP, 2032

×

Home Extension began a year ago when @11F made a 'legal shared space' within her 15sqm room through some ventilation tricks and opened it to us as a pub during lockdowns. The furnishing &sharing trend quickly spread throughout the whole building (btw, we live in a one-unit-per-floor pencil building on Tokyo Health's quarantine subsidies, just so you know how we were excited for this). The only person absent from our program is the 'ghost @13F' living upstairs...




[-2030-] 5.0 Technologies

5.0 TECHNOLOGIES

×

The COVID transitions of social production stimulate economic and political investments in the global network of 5.0 Infrastructures:

- Internet 5.0:
the pre-pandemic standard of 5G cellular network that has been reshaped and made globally available through a low-orbit satellite matrix under the US-China 5G competition, providing uninterrupted digital access for people at home and machines across borders;

- Industry 5.0:
a comprehensive system of remote collaboration between digital workers and machinery that enables fully unmanned industrial activities through automated procedures and remote human interventions; relevant technologies are initially developed to sustain the productivity of primary industries during COVID lockdowns and then been widely adopted in other industrial businesses of various scales for relevant social and economic transitions;

- Mobility 5.0:
the pre-pandemic visions of Level 5 autonomous vehicle that has been standardized for driverless forms of air, ground, and aquatic transportations, thanks to the restricted personal mobility that helps their training and legislation in most cities as well as heavy public investments in relevant services such as Amazon and Uber Air;

On the other hand, oppositions rise among the digitally disadvantaged societies and the stakeholders of human-labored industries with major concerns regarding employment opportunities, techno-monopolies, and cultural identities of production;

[-2035-] Nomads & COVID-Free Towns

NOMADS & COVID-FREE TOWNS

×

5.0 Technologies thrive across diverse industries and services under recurring COVID developments, promoting unmanned production lines on less-inhabitable lands and filling major transportation hubs with automated distribution facilities; the growing community of digital workers adopts the title of 'nomad' for spending most of their time and energy somewhere across screens while living elsewhere under necessary social distancing measures;

The uninterrupted productivity of 5.0 industries leverages the socio-economic status of nomads. Their significant buying powers converge into an offline leisure market as symbolized by COVID-free towns: gated touristy sites and enclosed portions of cities exclusively reserved for COVID-negative consumers to spend their money. These towns revitalize the pre-pandemic trends of digital nomads traveling around the world and add to the popular cultures of a larger population involved in digital economies.

[-2035-] Commuters & Labor Reservations

COMMUTERS & LABOR RESERVATIONS

×

In contrast to nomads living in and traveling between COVID-free towns, the majority of the world's urban populations remain under the title 'commuter' for having unavoidable commuting and interpersonal contacts in physical workplaces. The inability to perform comprehensive social distancing makes commuter societies perfect incubators for COVID mutations and variant developments, which in turn destabilize their social production and commonwealth. On the other hand, 5.0 technologies gradually drive out human-labored industries, often resulting in mass unemployment scenarios and violent conflicts in commuter societies.

While most developing countries rely on market protection acts against foreign digital invasions for their commuter populations to remain competitive within domestic and allied markets, digitally developed countries establish various forms of labor reservations with targeted health measures, tax benefits, and subsidies to human-labored industries and commuter populations in an effort of reconciling social conflicts between the two sides of digital divisions.

[2045--] Searise

SEARISE

×

The resumed industrial activities under 5.0 Technologies accelerate global warming progresses as emblemized by searise (a.k.a sea-level-rise). In addition to flooding waterfront settlements and damaging the urban infrastructures of coastal regions, searise further intensifies the COVID pandemic with millions of urban migrations (refugees) from coasts to inland cities and unexpected virus mutations as COVID meets the once-frozen pathogens from melted polar ice.

While nomad communities can escape from immediate searise impacts by traveling to safer COVID-free towns, their remote industrial activities (under limited searise awareness) push commuter societies into greater risks both socially and environmentally.

[2050] United Digital Nations

UNITED DIGITAL NATIONS

×

The 5.0 economies raise political tensions on cross-border laborforce, taxation, and market regulation, calling an invisible war against the geopolitical authorities on the global economy. In addition to speculative interventions of digital giants to domestic markets, digital turns also popularize tax-driven immigration and employment arbitrages among nomads which further dilute the notion of citizen and residency in social production;

The 2050 UN World Summit initiates United Digital Nations (UDN) as an intergovernmental division to reinforce geopolitical identities of digital economies through regulations on cross-border economic activities and corresponding jurisdictions; on the other hand, the interwoven interests between speculative politics and digital economies have been taking advantages of UDN interventions to build borderless capitals;
